The Nautilus Travel Guide
Will it be safe to travel to the Maldives given the current situation?

Part of the beauty and magic of The Nautilus Maldives is the Islands unparalleled location; set on its very own unspoiled and pristine white-sand Island and adorned with verdant endemic flora in the UNESCO Biosphere of the Baa Atoll. Boasting exclusivity and absolute privacy, the location of The Nautilus allows guests a greater sense of space and freedom there are no crowded places in this staggeringly beautiful slice of paradise. The private location also means that there is strict control of the entry of personnel into the Island which is not accessible to just anyone, and because The Nautilus is one of the smaller resorts in the Maldives, the number of guests, even at full capacity, is kept low, which helps to lessen the overall risk.

What is the Resorts cancellation policy in the event I choose to cancel my trip?

We understand that you may need to change your travel plans due to the unprecedented current situation. There are two options: you can postpone your booking to a later date, which will be subject to availability and any rate differences according to seasonal periods, or if you are still uncomfortable to travel, you can cancel your trip entirely.

Is it possible to book out the entire Island?

For those looking to reserve the entire Island for themselves and their loved ones, there are two exclusive Island buyout options available. This will allow you to enjoy the boundless freedom and luxury bohemian lifestyle of The Nautilus in complete privacy. For further information, please contact reservations@thenautilusmaldives.com or visit our information page here.

IS PCR TESTING AVAILABLE ON THE ISLAND? WHERE CAN I GET MORE INFORMATION?

We are here to help you with whatever query you might have. For more information regarding preventative measures and visiting us at this time, you can contact our General Manager via email at ashraf@thenautilusmaldives.com For the safety of everyone, the Health Protection Agency of the Maldives has made it mandatory for all inbound travellers to present a negative PCR test certificate on arrival. The test result should be not older than 72 hours. Kindly note that this does not apply to children below the age of 1 year. Maldives Immigration has introduced the Traveller Health Declaration which has to be filled and submitted by every traveller travelling to and from the Maldives. Guests with a history of contact with a suspected or confirmed case of COVID-19 within the past 14 days and/or persons who have a fever or respiratory symptoms such as cough, sore throat, shortness of breath within the past 14 days should not travel to the Maldives. It is recommended that you research the airline safety and hygiene measures, as well as their requirements when processing the booking.
